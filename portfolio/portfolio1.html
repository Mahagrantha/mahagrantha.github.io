Our operating principle

At Mahagrantha, every project starts on Day Zero.

Every document, just like the last one, starts from the same place: nobody has read it yet, and nobody owes it their attention. That assumption never goes away, no matter how long a product has existed.

This practice started the way most documentation problems do — with a product that worked, and a gap between what the team knew and what the user was expected to figure out on their own. Closing that gap, one guide at a time, is the whole job.

Eight-plus years in, the brief hasn't really changed. Understand the system, map who's reading and why, outline before drafting, then test it on someone who didn't build the product. Progress happens guide by guide, release by release — that's how documentation systems get built, and how they stay trustworthy.

If you're working with Mahagrantha, remember…

Documentation is read in a hurry, by someone who'd rather not be reading it at all.

Read under pressure

Not browsed for fun — opened mid-task, when something's already gone wrong.

Untested is a guess

If a real user hasn't walked through it, it's a draft pretending to be finished.

Structure first

Headings and steps before sentences — writing fills a shape, it doesn't invent one.

Plain beats clever

Clear, ordinary language is a feature of good docs, not a compromise on quality.

A repeatable process

How I work

01

Understand the system

Use the product myself, sit in on engineering standups, and interview the subject-matter experts who already know the gaps in the docs.

02

Map the audience and the job

Define who is reading, what they already know, and the single task they need to complete — before deciding on format or tone.

03

Outline before drafting

Structure first: headings, steps, and a consistent template, so the writing fills in a shape rather than inventing one as it goes.

04

Test, ship, and maintain

Run a usability pass and an SME review, publish, then track where readers drop off and feed that back into the next revision.
