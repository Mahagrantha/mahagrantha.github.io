CHAPTER 01

Introduction

Overview

La Poche is an integrated ecommerce management platform built for businesses that sell across multiple online marketplaces and sales channels. It brings sales, operations, finance, inventory, product management, and customer management into a single workspace, so teams stop reconciling the same order or stock count across five different tools.

Who this guide is for

This guide is written for the people who use La Poche day to day: sales and operations staff processing orders, inventory and warehouse staff managing stock, finance staff reconciling payments, and administrators managing users and permissions. Each chapter notes which role it's most relevant to.

What La Poche does

  • Manages products and inventory from one catalog
  • Synchronizes products, stock, and orders across connected marketplaces
  • Processes customer orders from receipt to delivery
  • Generates invoices and tracks payments and settlements
  • Monitors sales performance and produces business reports
BEFORE YOU START

You'll need an active La Poche account and a role assigned by your administrator. If you don't see a module mentioned in this guide, your role likely doesn't have access to it — see Chapter 9, User Management.

CHAPTER 02

Dashboard overview

Overview

The dashboard is the first screen you see after logging in. It's designed to answer one question at a glance: "is anything that needs my attention happening right now?"

Main features

Widget What it shows
Total Orders Order count for the selected date range, across all connected channels
Revenue Summary Gross revenue for the selected period, broken down by channel
Pending Deliveries Orders that are packed or shipped but not yet marked delivered
Inventory Status A live count of in-stock, low-stock, and out-of-stock products
Recent Transactions The latest payments, refunds, and settlements
Top Selling Products Best performers by units sold for the selected period
Marketplace Performance Orders and revenue compared across each connected channel

Reading the dashboard

  1. Log in with your registered email and password.
  2. Select a date range from the top-right filter — Today, This Week, This Month, or Custom.
  3. Review Pending Deliveries first; this is the queue most likely to need same-day action.
  4. Check Inventory Status for any product showing Low Stock or Out of Stock.
  5. Click any widget to drill into the full report behind it.

Troubleshooting

Issue Likely cause What to do
Dashboard numbers look out of date A connected marketplace hasn't synced recently Check sync status in Chapter 5, Marketplace Integration
Revenue Summary shows ₹0 for a known sales day Date range filter is set incorrectly Confirm the filter matches the period you expect
A widget is missing entirely Your role doesn't have permission to view it Ask an administrator to review your role under Chapter 9
CHAPTER 03

Product management

Overview

The Product Catalog is where every item you sell is defined once, then pushed out to every connected marketplace. Getting the catalog structure right here is what keeps listings consistent across channels.

Main features

  • Centralized product catalog shared across all connected channels
  • Structured fields for variants — color, size, and material
  • Bulk product image upload
  • Active / Inactive status to control marketplace visibility

Product structure

Each product record contains the following fields:

Field Description
Product Name Name of the product as shown to customers
SKU Unique product code used internally and across marketplaces
Category Product category, used for catalog navigation and reporting
Color Available color variant
Size Product dimensions or size variant
Price Selling price shown to customers
Stock Quantity Units currently available to sell
Product Images Product photographs shown on listings
Status Active (visible for sale) or Inactive (hidden from all channels)

Add a new product

  1. Go to Product Management → Product Catalog → Add Product.
  2. Enter the product name, category, and a unique SKU.
  3. Add variant details — color and size — if the product has more than one variant.
  4. Set the selling price and starting stock quantity.
  5. Upload at least one product image; marketplaces typically reject listings with none.
  6. Set Status to Active to make the product visible on every connected channel, or Inactive to keep it hidden while you finish setup.
  7. Save. La Poche queues the product for sync to all connected marketplaces.
TIP

Use a consistent SKU pattern (like JHB-001 for Jute Handbag) across your catalog — it makes inventory and sales reports far easier to scan later.

Troubleshooting

Issue Likely cause What to do
New product doesn't appear on a marketplace Sync hasn't completed, or the channel requires fields La Poche doesn't enforce Check sync status; confirm required fields for that marketplace are filled in
SKU error on save SKU is already in use by another product Choose a unique SKU — duplicates aren't allowed
Product shows wrong price on a channel A channel-specific price override is set Check Marketplace Integration → Price overrides for that channel
CHAPTER 04

Sales management

Overview

Orders placed on any connected marketplace flow into La Poche automatically. From here, one team can process every order the same way, regardless of which channel it came from.

Main features

  • Automatic order import from all connected marketplaces
  • A single workflow for order status, regardless of source channel
  • One-click invoice generation
  • Shipment tracking and returns management

Order workflow

Every order moves through the same six stages, in order:

Stage What it means
New Order Order received from a marketplace, not yet confirmed
Payment Confirmed Payment has cleared and the order is ready to fulfill
Processing Order is being picked and prepared
Packed Order is packed and ready for courier pickup
Shipped Order has left the warehouse with a tracking number assigned
Delivered Courier has confirmed delivery to the customer

Process a new order

  1. Open Sales Management → Orders and filter by status New Order.
  2. Open the order and confirm payment status matches what the marketplace reports.
  3. Move the order to Processing and generate the pick list.
  4. Once packed, mark the order Packed — this triggers the shipping label if you use an integrated courier.
  5. Enter the tracking number and mark the order Shipped.
  6. Generate the invoice from the order detail page; it's emailed to the customer automatically.
  7. When the courier confirms delivery — automatically or manually — mark the order Delivered.

Troubleshooting

Issue Likely cause What to do
Order stuck on New Order Payment hasn't been confirmed by the marketplace yet Wait for marketplace confirmation, or check the order directly on the source channel
Invoice missing GST details Customer or business tax details are incomplete Complete tax details under Finance Management before regenerating
Tracking number doesn't update status automatically Courier integration isn't connected for that shipment method Update status manually, or connect the courier under Marketplace Integration
CHAPTER 05

Marketplace integration

Overview

La Poche connects to the marketplaces you already sell on, so products, stock, and orders stay in sync without manual re-entry on each channel.

Supported channels

  • Amazon
  • Flipkart
  • Meesho
  • Shopify
  • WooCommerce
  • Own Website

Main features

  • Product synchronization — push catalog changes to every connected channel
  • Inventory synchronization — stock levels update everywhere in near real time
  • Order synchronization — orders import automatically as they're placed
  • Price updates — change a price once, apply it everywhere or per channel
  • Settlement tracking — match marketplace payouts to individual orders

Connect a new marketplace

  1. Go to Marketplace Integration → Connect Channel.
  2. Select the marketplace you want to connect from the supported list.
  3. Authorize La Poche using the credentials or API key requested by that marketplace.
  4. Choose whether to sync your full catalog immediately or select specific products.
  5. Confirm the sync — initial sync time depends on catalog size and may take up to a few hours for large catalogs.
WARNING

Disconnecting a marketplace does not cancel orders already placed there. Process any open orders on that channel before disconnecting.

Troubleshooting

Issue Likely cause What to do
Channel shows "Sync failed" Marketplace API credentials expired or were revoked Reconnect the channel and re-authorize
Stock count differs between La Poche and a marketplace Sync delay, or a manual stock edit made directly on the marketplace Trigger a manual sync; avoid editing stock outside La Poche where possible
Orders from a channel aren't importing Order sync is paused or the channel connection has expired Check connection status and reconnect if needed
CHAPTER 06

Inventory management

Overview

The Inventory module is the single source of truth for stock — every sale, return, and manual adjustment updates the same count, which is then reflected across every connected marketplace.

Main features

  • Add and adjust inventory manually or via bulk upload
  • View a full history of stock movements per product
  • Configure low-stock alerts per product
  • Generate inventory reports for any date range

Inventory status

Status Description
In Stock Product is available and visible for sale on connected channels
Low Stock Stock has fallen below the reorder threshold you've set
Out of Stock Product is unavailable and automatically hidden from sale

Set a low-stock alert

  1. Open Inventory Management and select the product.
  2. Open Stock Settings for that product.
  3. Enter a reorder threshold — the stock level that should trigger a Low Stock alert.
  4. Choose how you want to be notified: dashboard alert, email, or both.
  5. Save. The product moves to Low Stock automatically once stock crosses the threshold.

Troubleshooting

Issue Likely cause What to do
No low-stock alert received despite low stock Threshold not configured for that product Set a reorder threshold using the steps above
Stock count seems wrong after a return Return hasn't been processed through Sales Management yet Process the return in the order record; stock updates automatically
CHAPTER 07

Finance management

Overview

The Finance module handles everything money-related after an order is placed: invoicing, tax, payment tracking, refunds, and reconciling marketplace settlements.

Main features

  • Automatic invoice generation per order
  • GST calculation applied at the rate configured for each product category
  • Payment and refund tracking
  • Settlement reconciliation against marketplace payouts
  • Financial reports by period, channel, or product

How GST is calculated

La Poche applies the GST rate configured for a product's category to its selling price. For example:

Item Amount
Product Price ₹799
GST (18%) ₹143.82
Total Amount ₹942.82

Generate an invoice

  1. Open the order from Sales Management → Orders.
  2. Confirm the customer's billing details and GSTIN (if applicable) are correct.
  3. Select Generate Invoice from the order detail page.
  4. Review the calculated tax breakdown before confirming.
  5. Confirm — the invoice is generated and emailed to the customer automatically.

Troubleshooting

Issue Likely cause What to do
GST amount looks incorrect Product is assigned to the wrong tax category Correct the product's category under Product Management, then regenerate
Settlement doesn't match expected payout Marketplace commission or fees weren't accounted for Check the settlement breakdown report for itemized deductions
CHAPTER 08

Reports

Overview

Reports turn the day-to-day data in La Poche into something you can review weekly or monthly without digging through individual orders.

Available reports

Report What it answers
Sales Report How much did we sell, and when?
Product Performance Report Which products are selling well or underperforming?
Inventory Report What's the current stock position across the catalog?
Settlement Report Do marketplace payouts match what we expected?
Tax Report What GST have we collected, for filing purposes?
Customer Report Who's buying, and how often?

Generate a report

  1. Open Reports and select the report type.
  2. Choose a date range and, if relevant, filter by channel or category.
  3. Select Generate. Reports covering a long date range or large catalog may take a few minutes.
  4. Export to CSV or PDF, or view directly in the browser.

Troubleshooting

Issue Likely cause What to do
Report is empty for a period with known sales Date range or channel filter excludes the relevant data Widen the date range or remove channel filters and regenerate
Export is taking longer than expected Large date range or catalog size Narrow the date range, or wait — exports for large ranges can take several minutes
CHAPTER 09

User management

Overview

Administrators control who has access to La Poche and what each person can see and do, through roles.

Main features

  • Create and deactivate user accounts
  • Assign predefined roles to control access
  • Reset passwords on a user's behalf
  • Configure granular permissions per role

User roles

Role Typical access
Administrator Full access, including user management and system configuration
Sales Executive Order processing, customer records, and sales reports
Operations Executive Order fulfillment, shipping, and returns
Finance Executive Invoicing, payments, settlements, and financial reports
Warehouse Executive Inventory and stock management

Create a new user

  1. Go to User Management → Add User.
  2. Enter the user's name and work email.
  3. Assign a role from the table above — this determines which modules they can see.
  4. Save. The user receives an email invitation to set their password.
TIP

Assign the most restrictive role that still lets someone do their job. It's easier to grant additional access later than to audit over-permissioned accounts.

Troubleshooting

Issue Likely cause What to do
New user can't see a module they need Assigned role doesn't include that module Adjust their role or configure a custom permission
Invitation email never arrived Email address typo, or it landed in spam Confirm the email address and resend the invitation
