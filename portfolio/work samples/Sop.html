PART 1 · PROCEDURE 01

Order fulfillment

Covers the path an order takes from arriving in La Poche to being marked delivered, across all connected marketplaces.

Owner: Operations Executive Frequency: Daily, per order

From new order to delivery

Purpose

Ensure every order moves through the fulfillment workflow in the correct sequence, with no order left in new status for more than 24 hours.

Procedure
  1. Check the Orders dashboard each morning and filter by status new. New orders import automatically from all connected marketplaces.
  2. Confirm payment has cleared on the source marketplace. Move the order to payment_confirmed.
  3. Pick and pack the items. Move the order to processing, then packed once items are boxed and labeled.
  4. Hand the package to the courier and record the tracking number. Move the order to shipped — the tracking number is required at this step.
  5. Generate the invoice for the order and confirm it was emailed to the customer.
  6. Once the courier confirms delivery (via tracking or marketplace status), move the order to delivered.
COMMON MISTAKE

Status changes must follow the standard sequence — you cannot skip from new directly to shipped. Attempting to do so returns an invalid_status_transition error.

Escalation

If an order sits in new for more than 24 hours, or a marketplace shows a payment as cleared but La Poche doesn't reflect it, escalate to the Operations Lead.

PART 1 · PROCEDURE 02

Inventory & stock counts

Covers manual stock adjustments and the weekly physical count reconciliation.

Owner: Warehouse Executive Frequency: Weekly + ad hoc

Weekly stock reconciliation

Purpose

Keep the system's recorded stock quantity aligned with what's physically on the shelf, and prevent overselling on low-stock items.

Procedure
  1. Pull the current stock level for each SKU in the warehouse from the Inventory module.
  2. Physically count stock for each SKU during the Friday count window.
  3. Where the physical count differs from the system count, apply a manual stock adjustment with a reason of recount.
  4. For damaged or written-off stock found during the count, apply a separate adjustment with reason damage.
  5. Review any SKU flagged low_stock or out_of_stock and confirm whether the reorder threshold needs updating.
  6. Log the count summary (SKUs adjusted, total variance) in the weekly ops report.
TIP

A stock adjustment's delta should be positive to add stock and negative to remove it — always double-check the sign before submitting.

PART 1 · PROCEDURE 03

Returns & refunds

Covers handling a returned item once it's back in the warehouse.

Owner: Operations Executive + Finance Executive Frequency: As needed

Processing a returned item

Procedure
  1. Inspect the returned item against the reason given by the customer (wrong item, damaged, changed mind, etc.).
  2. If the item is resellable, apply a stock adjustment with reason return to add it back to available inventory.
  3. If the item is damaged beyond resale, apply a stock adjustment with reason damage instead, so it isn't counted as sellable.
  4. Hand off to Finance to process the refund through the originating marketplace's refund flow.
  5. Finance confirms the refund against the original invoice and notes it in the settlement record for that period.
COMMON MISTAKE

Don't add a damaged return back to stock with reason return — it will show as sellable and risks being shipped to the next customer.

PART 1 · PROCEDURE 04

New product listing

Covers adding a new product to the catalog and getting it live across connected marketplaces.

Owner: Sales Executive Frequency: As needed

Listing a new product

Procedure
  1. Confirm the product has a unique SKU. The system will reject a duplicate with a sku_conflict error.
  2. Create the product with name, category, price, and starting stock quantity. Leave status as inactive until photography and copy are ready.
  3. Add variant details (color, size) where applicable.
  4. Once content is ready, set status to active. This queues a sync to every connected marketplace.
  5. Check the Marketplaces dashboard the next day to confirm the listing synced successfully on each channel.
TIP

Keep new listings inactive until they're fully ready — switching status later re-triggers the marketplace sync rather than requiring a manual re-list.

PART 1 · PROCEDURE 05

Marketplace sync recovery

Covers what to do when a connected channel shows sync_failed.

Owner: Operations Executive Frequency: As needed

Recovering a failed sync

Procedure
  1. Check the Marketplaces dashboard for any channel showing sync_failed and note the last_synced_at timestamp.
  2. Trigger a manual sync for the affected channel. This runs asynchronously, so don't trigger it repeatedly while one is in progress — a second attempt returns sync_in_progress.
  3. Wait at least 10 minutes, then refresh the Marketplaces dashboard to confirm the channel now shows connected.
  4. If the channel still shows sync_failed after a manual retry, check whether the marketplace's OAuth connection has expired — this requires reconnecting through the dashboard, not the API.
  5. If reconnecting doesn't resolve it, escalate to the Operations Lead with the channel name and timestamp of the first failure.
PART 1 · PROCEDURE 06

Settlement reconciliation

Covers matching marketplace settlement payouts against expected order totals.

Owner: Finance Executive Frequency: Monthly

Monthly settlement close

Procedure
  1. Pull the settlement list for the closing month, filtered by channel.
  2. For each settlement record, compare the payout amount against the sum of order invoices for that period and channel.
  3. Flag any variance beyond the agreed tolerance (e.g. commission or fee mismatches) for review.
  4. Request the relevant sales or tax report (type settlement or tax) for the period to support the reconciliation.
  5. Once reconciled, sign off the period in the finance tracker and archive the supporting reports.
PART 1 · PROCEDURE 07

User onboarding & offboarding

Covers granting and revoking platform access for team members.

Owner: Administrator Frequency: As needed

Onboarding a new user

Procedure
  1. Confirm the new team member's role: Administrator, Sales Executive, Operations Executive, Finance Executive, or Warehouse Executive.
  2. Create the user account with their name, work email, and assigned role. An invitation email is sent automatically.
  3. Confirm they've accepted the invitation and can log in.
  4. If they need API access, generate a key under their account — note that it inherits the permissions of their role.
Owner: Administrator Frequency: As needed

Offboarding a departing user

Procedure
  1. Deactivate the user's account on their last working day. This immediately revokes any API keys generated under it.
  2. Confirm with the relevant team lead that any scheduled reports or integrations tied to that person's account are reassigned.
  3. Remove them from any external tools tied to platform access (Slack channels, shared drives, etc.).
IMPORTANT

Deactivation is immediate and revokes API keys right away — confirm any automated jobs running under that user's key have been moved to a service account first.
